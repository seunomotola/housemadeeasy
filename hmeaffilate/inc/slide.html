Root Causes in Hospital Settings

Multifactorial Genesis of DDIs

System Factors

  • Multiple prescribers without comprehensive review
  • High-stress, fast-paced environment
  • Frequent care transitions (ICU to ward)
  • Shift changes and handoffs

Patient Factors

  • Advanced age
  • Multiple comorbidities
  • Reduced organ function
  • Complex medication regimens

The "Prescribing Cascade"

Specialists prescribe medications without a comprehensive review of the patient's complete drug profile, increasing oversight likelihood

Pharmacist-Led Interventions

Multifaceted Approach to Prevention

üéØ Core Strategy: Systematic screening and intervention at multiple stages of the medication-use process
  • Prospective Order Review: Screening all new medication orders against existing profile using clinical decision support and expert judgment
  • Interprofessional Rounds: Active participation in treatment discussions, offering alternatives and monitoring recommendations
  • Medication Reconciliation: Documenting home medications, reconciling across transitions to identify discrepancies
  • Therapeutic Drug Monitoring: Recommending and interpreting serum levels, monitoring for early toxicity signs
  • Patient Education: Counseling on medication risks and signs of adverse interactions at discharge

Critical Transition Points

Medication Reconciliation at Key Junctures

Transitions of care are the most vulnerable periods for medication errors, including overlooked DDIs

üè• Admission

  • Detailed patient interview for best possible medication history (BPMH)
  • Uncover undocumented medications and supplements
  • Identify pre-existing long-term DDIs
  • Establish accurate baseline

üö™ Discharge

  • Reconcile pre-admission, in-hospital, and discharge medications
  • Resolve unintended discrepancies
  • Prevent new DDIs from continuing post-discharge
  • Explicit follow-up planning (e.g., INR monitoring for warfarin + amiodarone)
Result: Prevents post-discharge adverse events and reduces hospital readmissions
